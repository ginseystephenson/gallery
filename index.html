Squires Treess

The trees right outside Squires Student Center stand tall on a sunny afternoon. The cool breeze calms the nerves of stressed college students sitting outside Au Bon Pain as they do anything expect study for finals.

Bald knob

My friends Julis (left) and Melissa (right) gazing upon the vast valley in front of them on a sunrise hike at Bald Knob. As they stare among the clouds, they begin to contemplate their life decisions because they remember they have to take all of their finals.

the one and only burruss Hall

One of the most recognizable buildings at Virginia Tech, Burruss Hall is an iconic landmark for VT students. Burruss is significant to me because I stand outside it every day waiting for the bus in the cold weather, and it is miserable.

Thinking on the drillfield

My friend Julia sitting on the drillfield and thinking about the good old days. By the good old days, I am referencing to when she lived on campus and did not have to deal with Virginia Tech Parking because it is the worst.

Drillfield Trees

The trees on the drillfield are beautiful. During full, they change a beautiful orange and maroon, and remind me why I love Virginia Tech. Sometimes you can find students enoing between them.

street of mixed emotions

I drive down this road every day to get home from school. Oftentimes, the road is empty and I can drive a solid 5 over the speed limit. Other days, I get stuck behind a minivan going 10 mph. I have mixed feelings about this street.

The Pylons: A love story

The pylons are a staple to Virginia Tech. They are a great place to watch the sunset, and truly bask in the Virginia Tech environment. And no matter what, there will always be that one couple sitting directly in the middle, holding hands and getting in the way of the view.

Red Bush

The red bush provided a pop of color to my repertoire of photos. I enjoyed the vibrancy from an otherwise dull area on campus.

Trees to Hokie Grill

These trees pave the way to Hokie Grill. I love to eat Chick-fil-a, and these trees lead me directly there. They also provide a nice shady area during the hotter months.

Tree tops

I love the way the sun hits these trees. They illuminate multiple shades of green against the light blue sky. The colors blend to create a beautiful image in nature.
